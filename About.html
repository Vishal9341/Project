Our Story

How Food Share began and the journey so far

Bridging the Gap Between Surplus and Need

Food Share was founded in 2025 when our founders witnessed the heartbreaking paradox of food waste amidst hunger. While working at local restaurants, they saw perfectly good food being discarded daily, while shelters and community kitchens struggled to feed those in need.

This realization sparked the creation of Food Share - a platform designed to efficiently connect food donors with organizations that serve vulnerable communities. What started as a small local initiative has now grown into a nationwide movement.

Today, we partner with hundreds of restaurants, cafes, hotels, and event venues across the country, helping them redirect surplus food to where it's needed most, while reducing their environmental footprint.

Our Mission

We aim to build a hunger-free society by using technology to create a smart and efficient food sharing network. Our platform enables restaurants, cafes, and event organizers to easily donate surplus food, while nearby NGOs are instantly notified to collect and distribute it safely to those in need.

We're committed to reducing food waste while addressing food insecurity, creating a sustainable solution that benefits both our environment and our communities.

Our Vision

We envision a world where no food is wasted, and no one goes to bed hungry. By connecting communities through compassion and technology, we hope to inspire people to share more and waste less.

Our long-term goal is to expand our network globally, creating a world where surplus food is automatically redirected to those who need it, eliminating both hunger and food waste in our lifetime.

How Food Share Works

Our simple three-step process to reduce food waste and fight hunger

Streamlining Food Redistribution

1. Donation Listing: Food providers use our simple platform to list surplus food available for donation, specifying type, quantity, and pickup time.

2. Smart Matching: Our algorithm instantly notifies nearby partner NGOs about available donations, matching them based on capacity, location, and needs.

3. Efficient Distribution: NGOs collect the food and distribute it to communities in need through their established networks, with full transparency and tracking.

Our technology ensures that food moves quickly from donor to recipient, maintaining quality and safety while maximizing impact.
